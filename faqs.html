Why should I use a Master Electrician?

Master Electricians Australia is the national body that sets the benchmark for excellence in the electrical contracting industry. Members are required to demonstrate and maintain a high standard of skills, safety practices, and industry knowledge. Being a member means our team has access to expert industry advice and ongoing training, and that our work is held to a standard above the minimum licensing requirement.

For more information: masterelectricians.com.au/consumers/use-master-electrician

Why do I need a licensed electrician for electrical work?

All electrical work in NSW must be carried out by a licensed electrician. Unlicensed work is illegal, unsafe, and can void your home insurance. In our experience, jobs done by unlicensed tradespeople or DIY attempts almost always end up costing the client more to fix than if they had called a licensed electrician in the first place.

Safety Checks and Compliance

When should I get an electrical safety check?

We recommend getting an electrical safety check in any of the following situations:

  • When you purchase a property
  • If your property is more than 25 years old
  • If it has been more than five years since your last check
  • If you are experiencing anything unusual, such as tingling or shocks when touching appliances, taps, or showers
  • If you have concerns about electrical work carried out in the past
  • If you are unsure whether your property meets current electrical wiring and safety standards

What does electrical compliance mean?

Electrical compliance means your installation has been completed to the current Australian Wiring Rules, is fitted with the appropriate safety devices including RCBOs (safety switches) and hardwired smoke alarms, and has the required earthing and bonding system in place. Each of these has a direct impact on the safety of anyone using the property.

How do I know if my electrical system is compliant?

Your system is likely compliant if it follows the current wiring standard (AS/NZS 3000:2018), if your property is less than 10 years old, or if all electrical work was completed after 2018. Properties more than 10 years old have a higher chance of falling short of specific compliance requirements. If you are unsure, a safety check is the most straightforward way to find out.

What should I do if my safety switch keeps tripping?

A safety switch that trips repeatedly is telling you something is wrong. It may be a faulty appliance, an overloaded circuit, or a wiring issue. Do not ignore it or simply reset it without investigating further. Call a licensed electrician to identify the cause.

Smoke Alarms

How often should I get my smoke alarm tested?

Every 12 months. To test your smoke alarm yourself:

  • Press the test button
  • Clean the unit and inspect it for damage
  • Replace the battery if needed
  • Check the manufacturing date. Smoke alarms more than 10 years old should be replaced.

A licensed electrician should carry out a full compliance check, particularly for hardwired alarms in tenanted or commercial properties.
